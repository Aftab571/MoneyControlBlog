Welcome to the world of Money control

Spending money is easier than earning it. Also, taking control over it is most of the times a challenge. Budget Book is designed for the purpose of taking charge of your expenditure.

It allows the user to do the following:

  • Enable the users to maintain a healthy budget
  • Let the users know their areas of excess spending.
  • Let the users decide if their spending’s are worth it.
  • Alert the user if their income is spent at a higher rate

The Application is designed with a vision of optimizing the expenditure of the user by keeping a track of spend habits and suggesting efficient corrective methods. The main aims of the application are:

  • Enable the user to input his/her expenditure under various categories of spend.
  • To analyse the spend habits of the user and generate monthly reports depecting the spend trends.
  • Based on the analysis of the spend trends, help the user to analyse the areas of excess spending.



INTERESTING FACTS AND FIGURES.

Before starting any development process, it is very important to carry out extensive Market research to understand problems. A few interesting facts are stated below.
  • An average typical American student in high school has about 13 outstanding credit obligations
  • According to Employee Benefit Research Institute survey, only 14 percent of American workers say that they are "very confident" that they will have enough money to live comfortably in retirement and 30 percent of workers said they have less than $1,000 in savings and investments
  • People who use a credit/debit cards to pay, they spend on average of about 50 percent more than when they pay with cash

On the basis of above facts and figures and issues faced by people pertaining to their budgets, our team found out that It is essential that there exists a platform where a user can track and keep a check on his monthly expenditure and savings and hence our team decided to develop a n application which would not only help the user have a log of his transactions but also allow him to visualize his expenditure depending on which he can access each area of expenditure and decides where he needs to have a check and how to do it.


WHY IS THERE A NEED OF MONEY CONTROL?

Money Control is an interesting topic to discuss upon. Different people have different reasons and different goals for which they save up money over a period of time. However, one common thing is that everyone needs money in this 21st centaury so that they can lead a comfortable and worry free life. Hence, it is important that people have some tools through which they can keep a track on their money. Also, People tend to spend more when they don’t have a proper visualization of the data. Many people find it confusing at the end of the month or the year when they try to calculate their expenditure or saving and tend to get frustrated and leave the task all together. Once this happens it is impossible for the user to analyze his area of surplus spending and evaluate the areas which provide him/her an option to save by cost cutting methods. Based on all these situations, there is dire need to keep in check our finances for a healthy budget. Based on the above statements, we have a vision of developing an app which would enable the users to maintain a healthy budget. Let the user know their areas of spending and let them visualize and analysis their data so that they can maintain a healthy budget.

REQUIREMENT ANALYSIS AND SPECIFICATIONS

Requirement analysis for our application ‘Budget Book’ which aims at helping the user to manage his expenses was carried out in two steps. In the first step we approached the customer and by a thorough discussion we understood his requirements, expectations and the important basic features necessary for the application. Once the initial discussion was done, a discussion among the team members took place on how to arrive at the requirements of the user and if there are any additional features apart from that specified by the customer that would further enhance the application. The requirements gathered from the Customer were grouped under two categories. The first category consists of the features absolutely necessary for the basic foundation of the application. The features which would consist of the core idea implementation and the basic reason as to why the application was developed at the first place. The second category consists of features and implementations which would enhance the application and make it market ready or the features which if implemented would then promote the app to release in the play store In the second step of gathering requirements each team member thought as an independent user as to what features would be necessary in the above said application. Each team members came out with varied features some of which were essentials and others which would not be of more important to the actual users.


USER STORIES

All the requirements gathered from above two steps were formulated as user stories. The user stories are numbered according to the priority of the requirement. That is the stories at the top are the features which are of at most necessary and followed by features which would enhance the application as stated above. The user stories are as follows:
  • As a user, I want to manage my expenses by using an application which would record my Income and expenditure and help me analyze my budget.
  • As a user, I would like to have a Username and password so that nobody can have a peek at my personal budget.
  • As a user, I would like to select the Month followed by year of the financial details.
  • As a user, I would like to enter my Income only once and have an option of changing it as and when required.
  • As a user, I should be able to enter my fixed expenses such as rent, loans, electricity bill, insurance etc only once and would like to have a drop down menu for various categories and include them in my budget every month.
  • As a user, I would like to have a feature where I could specify my unfixed expenses in the various categories such as food, entertainment, and movies etc, state a comment on the transaction and state the mode of payment and have a limit for the same.
  • As a user, I would like to have a blank comment box in the unfixed section where I can enter any category of unfixed expense which is not previously present.
  • As a user, I would like to have a numeric keypad instead of a QWERTY type where ever I enter numeric values.
  • As a user I would like to have different sections for entering my Income and expenses so that it is not confusing at the end of the day.
  • As a user, I would like to have a dashboard which consists of all the features of the application.
  • As a user, I would like to select the currency type.
  • As a user, I would like to have instructions to use the application.
  • As a user, I would like to have filters for my transactions where I could view my budget for particular criteria.
  • As a user, I would like to have visual representation of the data which make analysis easier.

WRONG ASSUMPTION IDENTIFICATION AND RESOLVING


While gathering requirement analysis there were also certain areas where we went overboard with our assumptions which were quite unnecessary. The following are the assumptions which we made:
  • We thought the app should take the email-id of the user to sign up for the application which was then rendered redundant after discussion with the TA as the application doesn’t require any unique identification and since the app is a offline one.
  • Continuing with the above point, we thought that the application should be online so that the user data can be stored in the central database and can be used to give user an analysis by comparing the data with national average and help user manage the budget. However, after discussion with the TA it was concluded that not many people are comfortable with sharing their personal budget information online and this would be a negative impact on the application.
  • As discussed in the previous point, the team had thought of having a central database to store data of all users. However, the implementation of the same was found to be unnecessary and at the same time expensive after discussion with TA.

Design Pattern

In software engineering, a design pattern is a general repeatable solution to a commonly occurring problem in software design. A design pattern isn't a finished design that can be transformed directly into code. It is a description or template for how to solve a problem that can be used in many different situations. We have used the following design patterns in our application.

  • MVC – Model View Controller

    MVC Pattern stands for Model-View-Controller Pattern. This pattern is used to separate application's concerns.

    • Model - Model represents an object carrying data. It can also have logic to update controller if its data changes.
    • View - View represents the visualization of the data that model contains.
    • Controller - Controller acts on both model and view. It controls the data flow into model object and updates the view whenever data changes. It keeps view and model separate.
  • Interface

    • It is a collection of abstract methods. A class implements an interface thereby inheriting the abstract methods of inheritance by which we can enforce the method usage.

  • Serializable

    • Converts objects or data into byte code and transfers it from one activity to another activity.

  • Custom Pattern

    • There was a repeatable task of creating table in each activity for displaying the components added by the user. The method of design pattern accepts the data list, module name, table layout and context. We have used a custom pattern of creating tables in the required activities.

SPECIFIC CONTENT OF USE

Student

A student is always tight on time and budget. Student, especially who stay abroad always have a constrain on expenditure since they have to juggle between a lot of things which vary from basic necessities such as Food, shelter, clothing, Internet to educational needs such as text books, stationeries and other extra expenditures. Hence, it is very necessary that a student always has a way to somehow keep a track of her/his budget, be informed if at all she/he is over his monthly allowance. A more traditional way is to write down all the expenses one has occurred over the month, sum it up and see if she/he is below his allowance or at least equal to it. However, this may be very robust at times. Also, one tends to forget a few expenses over the period and there is no way to match if she/he is over his budget except for of course her/his inner conscious which somehow always losses in front of one’s greed. Budget book is an application, which not only helps a student to keep a track of his expenditure over the time, but it also helps her/him to set monthly limits for various expenditure and ones he reaches a certain threshold she/he is alerted and by this way he can always keep a track. It also helps him visualize the expenses in a pictorial form and also as a downloadable summary which he can always analyze to cut down a few things and stay on budget.


A Working Individual

A working individual, be it a Doctor, an Engineer, an Architect, a Dancer, a Professor or in any other field is always a little busy to just keep a track of his incomes and expenditure. She/he may have various sources of income apart from their income from professional work. Sometimes, it just gets very frustrating to track the inflow and outflow of cash when you have so many incomes and a lot more expenditures especially when you have a family. Hence it is necessary that one can keep an eye on the inflow and outflow. Also, for a few individuals it’s annoying to go through numbers all the time, hence visualization also comes into picture. An Application which can not only help them keep a track of each sources of income and expenditure, along with the time stamp and the mode of expenditure is a very handy tool and that is what budget book is aimed at. An individual can enter his various sources of income, expenditure; methods of expenditure, monthly limits on unfixed expenditure and at the end of the month can visualize all the cash flow and analyze it.

Software Testing


As the Beta prototype is now ready its time to test our application regressively and get to know the critical, major and minor errors before the end user uses it and makes fun of it or provides negative feedback on the social media. In order to test the App and also to improve the performance & responsiveness of the app we applied the methods of both White Box and Black box testing in our App.

White Box Testing

White box testing methods are applied when the internal working or structure of the code is known. It is mostly performed by the Developer to know the critical bugs, redundat code, to test the actual scenario for which the is intented to do.

With the help of White box testing we can get to know the code coverage which include the following:

  • Class Coverage
  • Method Coverage
  • Line Coverage
  • Statement Coverage

Application of White Box Testing approach on Budget Book

  • In order to test the internal structure of our application we used jUnit4 along with robolectric framework.
  • Robolectric framework was used so that we can mock the application context without running the actual application.

Black Box Testing

It is a type of software testing method in which the internal structure/ design/ implementation of the item being tested is NOT known to the tester.

Type of Black Box testing:

  • Equivalence Partitioning.
  • Boundary value Analysis.
  • Decision table.
  • State transition
  • Exploratory testing
  • Error guessing.

Equivalence Class Partitioning:

In this we divide test conditions in groups. From each group we test only one condition. If a condition from a group work then all the conditions from that group work

In our Application we have identified the following as the groups:

  1. Amount fields:

    Splitting the test conditions as: Entering numeric value, entering alphanumeric, entering alphabets, entering -ve values, entering decimal values.

  2. Date fields manual entry:

    Splitting the test conditions as: Entering dd-mm-yyyy, entering alphanumeric, entering alphabets, entering special characters, Entering different format combinations (MM-dd-yyyy | yyyy-dd-MM...)

Boundary Value Analysis:

In our application we identified the following under the Boundary value analysis.

  1. Amount fields :

    valid: >0 & maxlength 12 invalid <=0 2. valid

  2. Date fields :

    valid calendar date (Ex: leap year), invalid: Invalid calendar date (ex: 31-04-2019)

Decision Table:

This test is appropriate for functionalities which has logical relationships between inputs i.e; if else condition. Using this technique we deal with combination of inputs.

State Transition:

Using state transition we pick test cases from an application where we need to test different system transitions. We can apply this when an application gives a different output for the same input depending on what has changed in the earlier state.

Exploratory testing:

We performed this testing to explore all the functionalities and detect any serious issues.

Error guessing:

We performed this testing along with equivalence partitioning and boundary value analysis. Mostly form submission were testing where we already expected the desired results.

Black Box Testing on Budget Book

In our application testing we tried to cover as many types of testing as possible in the below mentioned user stories.

User Stories

  1. As a user I want to create a PIN for the application and also login using correct pin.

    In this user story we verify if the user is able to insert a 4 digit PIN in the input field and log into the application. On opening the application the first activity for new user is creating PIN for the application to secure the information in the application. Upon creating the PIN the user should be navigated to the income activity. We have tested this scenario by creating PIN as a new user and provided income details in the income field and date field. This pin only accepts numeric vales and alphabets, alphanumeric special characters are not accepted. We have tested this user story as a part of exploratory testing by providing incorrect pin, providing no pin and providing correct pin comparing the results with the expected results.

  2. As a user I want to add my monthly income.

    In this user story we have tested income activity of the application for the income field and date field which are mandatory in the income activity. We have tested the fields by not providing mandatory information and see how the application is behaving also by providing incorrect information in the date field and income field. In the income field we tested for boundary value analysis by providing the maximum number of income that is 13 digits as it is not accepted beyond 13 digits also null value is not accepted as it throws an error. For equivalence class partitioning in income field we have tested by providing zero value, negative value and positive value and compared the results with the expected results to check the testing In the date field we have performed equivalence partitioning by providing the valid date, invalid date and special characters in the date field. By providing the dates that exist and dates that don’t exist we have performed boundary value analysis testing for this user story.

  3. As a user I want to add my unfixed expenditure

    In this user story we have tested what happens when mandatory fields are not provided as a part of exploratory testing. We have provided with all types of inputs available and checked how the inputs affect the output of the activity. In the expenditure field we have tested with providing the positive values, zero and negative values as a part of equivalence partitioning test. For positive flow test we have provided with positive values of expenditure and for negative flow test we have provided zero and negative values. And as part of exploratory testing we have explored the fields of comment and payment method. Comment field accepts numeric values, alphabets, alphanumeric and special characters. Payment method accepts the methods that are provided in the dropdown.

  4. As a user I want to visualize Expenditure

    This user story is majorly focused on exploratory testing as from date and to date field is already tested in the income activity as part of equivalence partitioning testing and boundary value analysis. So in this activity we have explored all the inputs available in the filtering section and checked the outputs. We have used different timeframes available, the frequency of expenses, and the method of payment as inputs in the filtering activity to attain desirable outputs.

  5. As a user I want to reset my pin.

    This user story is focused on changing the PIN set by the user to a new PIN. In this activity we have used the testing methods of error guessing testing and exploratory testing. By providing inputs we are trying to guess the output of the activity such as by providing wrong pin in the reset activity and trying to guess the error that will be displayed on the screen, providing the same pin for both the old and new pin and guessing the error and providing the correct inputs and checking whether the pin is changed message is displayed or not. Also as a part of exploratory testing we are checking what happens to the output when different inputs are provided such as wrong old pin, providing same pin for both the old and new pin, providing correct old pin and resetting the new pin, also with no inputs in the old pin field and vice versa.
